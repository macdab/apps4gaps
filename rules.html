Rules

How to take part in the competition?

1. Introduction

Apps4Gaps is an All Ireland competition aimed at encouraging young people to develop ideas and create applications that will provide innovative and fresh ways of exploiting the Open Data freely available from the Census 2011 that could benefit society in such areas as transport, housing, planning, education, communications and health.

In the process, participants will learn or increase their skills in computer programming, utilise the opportunities presented by Open Data, and be challenged in creating technologies such as mobile apps that can exploit the economic and social material presented.

2. Partners

The Central Statistics Office (CSO), the INSIGHT Research Centre for Data Analytics, NCTE, Project Maths, the Digital Repository Ireland (DRI) and Coderdojo have teamed up to launch the All Ireland Apps4Gaps competition.

3. Key Competition Dates

  • October 17th - Launch of Apps4Gaps
  • January 31st - Closing date for Registration of projects. Projects have to be registered by 17.00 on that date.
  • April 11th - Closing date for Submission of entries. Projects have to be received by 17.00 on that date.
  • May 16th- Public announcement of winners.

Eligibility

Student Category

This category is open to teams of two to three people who are attending, or are registered in, a primary school, a secondary school or a third level college located on the island of Ireland and who reside legally on the island of Ireland. Teams can enter the competition through a submission of a registration form signed by a teacher, lecturer or course advisor/instructor from the school or college that they are attending.

Open Category

This category is open to individuals or organisations that reside legally or are registered on the island of Ireland. Teams of up to three people with a designated leader are eligible to submit apps.

Participation of multiple teams from the same organization is allowed. However individual members can only participate once in the competition.

Project Groups

Completed projects can be submitted in one of two groups:

  • Concept entry: Proposal of ideas for theoretical software applications (“apps”)
  • Working app: Submission of a fully developed app

Definition of an app

An app is defined as any piece of software that can be run on a mobile device (mobile app), within a social platform (social app), or in a browser (Web app).

Datasets

All project entrants must use at least one of the required CSO Datasets from the App4Gaps data catalogue.

Publicity/Display of Submissions

Except where prohibited, participation in the Apps4Gaps competition constitutes competition participants consent to Apps4Gaps and/or its partners/sponsors for use of the applicant’s name, likeness, photograph, and/or college/workplace for promotional purposes in any media, worldwide, without further consideration.

Evaluation Criteria

  • Data Use
  • Alignment with Project Goals
  • Functionality and Implementation
  • Usefulness to students, teachers, schools and citizens of Ireland
  • Feasibility
  • Creativity/Originality

Adjudication

The decision of the judges on the selection of winners in the Apps4Gaps competition is final and conclusive and is not subject to appeal.

Licence and Limits of Liability

Participants must comply with the official competition rules. Failure to do so will result in disqualification from the competition.

All submissions must be the original work of the participant(s), solely owned by the person or team making the Entry and have not been entered into any other competition nor violate the intellectual property, common law or privacy rights of other parties.

Submission of an Entry

All entries made be submitted through the Apps4Gaps website www.apps4gaps.ie Registration of all entries has to be completed online by 17.00 on January 31st. Submissions have to be completed at http://apps4gaps.ie/register/ by 17.00 on April 11th.

The following information is required for a valid submission:

    For a Concept only submission entry:
  1. Step 1: Name/Title. Create a name/title for the app
  2. Step 2: Project Overview. Write a paragraph that summarises the role of the app and how it works.
  3. Step 3: Narrative. Create a narrative (user manual). Write up to five pages that explain how the app works and how to use it, the dataset used from Census 2011, the target audience and the benefits of the app to such an audience.
  4. Step 4: If entry is a Concept entry, skip to Step 8. Links to apps, photographs or videos are not required for Concept entries.
Where the submission is a Working app, the following are needed in addition to the Concept-only entry requirments:
  1. Step 5: App. Create the app and post it to a hosted site or app store.
  2. Step 6: Video. Create a video demonstrating the working app and post to a video hosting site such as www.youtube.com. (You will be asked to provide the link to the video on the submission form).
  3. Step 7: Photograph. Create a photograph of your app suitable for public posting.
  4. Step 8: Submission Form. Fill out the submission form (available for download at www.apps4gaps.ie). In the places indicated, upload the narrative as well as the photograph and provide links to the hosted app and to the video of the Working app.

An individual acting as a project team leader, in either the Student or Open categories, must ensure that he/she as well as the other team members meet the eligibility requirements for individual participants.

  1. Step 9: Finalize. Finalize your submission by clicking the “Submit” button. (All applications must be submitted via the Apps4Gaps website no later than 17.00 on April 11th. Submission of an entry is an acknowledgement that the Entrant has complied with all competition requirements stated or implied. No awards will be granted prior to verification that entries comply with all competition requirements. Failure to comply with competition requirements will result in disqualification.
